Indian Literary Heritage

Rabindranath Tagore (1861–1941)

Who he was:

Tagore was a great Indian poet, writer, and thinker. He was born in Bengal and is best known for poems, songs, and stories.

Famous for:

Wrote India’s national anthem and won the Nobel Prize in 1913. His writing emphasized love, nature, and spirituality.

Other work:

Founded Shantiniketan, later Visva-Bharati University.

Munshi Premchand (1880–1936)

Who he was:

Famous Hindi-Urdu novelist known for social realism. Born in Uttar Pradesh.

Famous for:

Known as "Upanyas Samrat", he wrote about caste, poverty, and rural life. Key works: *Godaan*, *Kafan*.

Sarojini Naidu (1879–1949)

Who she was:

Renowned poet and Indian freedom fighter. Known as the "Nightingale of India".

Famous for:

Her poetry celebrated India’s landscape and emotions. Famous works include *The Golden Threshold* and *The Bird of Time*.

Other work:

She was the first woman to become the President of the Indian National Congress.

Mahadevi Varma (1907–1987)

Who she was:

A key poet of the Chhayavad (romanticism) movement in Hindi literature.

Famous for:

Her poetry expressed deep emotion, womanhood, and spirituality. Known for *Yama*, which won the Jnanpith Award.

Other work:

She was also an educationist and editor of *Chand* magazine, a pioneer in women's rights.
