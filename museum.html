Famous Museums of India

National Museums

Overview: One of the largest museums in India, featuring collections from pre-historic times to modern artworks.

History: Foundation stone laid by Jawaharlal Nehru in 1949; opened to the public in 1960.

Best Time: October to March.

Specialities: Harappan artifacts, bronze sculptures, miniature paintings.

Nearby Attractions: India Gate, Rashtrapati Bhavan.

Food: Saravana Bhavan (South Indian), Andhra Bhavan Canteen.

Restaurants: Bukhara, Indian Accent.

Sweets: Daulat ki Chaat, Lassi from Bengali Market.

Best Time: November to February.

Nearby Attractions: Maidan, Indian Museum, Kalighat Temple.

Archaeological Collections

Overview: A vast collection from different parts of the world including rare European and Asian artifacts.

History: Collection curated by Nawab Salar Jung III; inaugurated in 1951.

Best Time:October to February.

Specialities:Veiled Rebecca, jade collection, Persian carpets.

Nearby Ttractions: Charminar, Mecca Masjid, Chowmahalla Palace.

Food: Shadab Hotel, Paradise Biryani.

Restaurants: Bawarchi, Café Bahar.

Sweets: Qubani ka Meetha, Irani Chai.

Overview: Repository of Buddhist cultural heritage and ancient sculptures.

History: Established in 1910; displays the Ashokan Lion Capital (India’s emblem).

Best Time: November to February.

Specialities: Mauryan and Gupta era sculptures, Buddhist relics.

Nearby Attractions: Dhamek Stupa, Varanasi Ghats, Mulagandha Kuti Vihar.

Food: Local Tibetan and Indian food stalls.

Restaurants: Kashi Chaat.

Sweets: Malaiyo, Rabri, Lemon Tea.

Overview: A window into the maritime activities of the Indus Valley Civilization.

History: Excavations began in the 1950s; museum showcases ancient dockyard remains.

Best Time: October to March.

Specialities: Beads, terracotta ornaments, scale models of city layouts.

Nearby Attractions: Modhera Sun Temple, Nal Sarovar Bird Sanctuary.

Food: Kathiyawadi cuisine, street vendors.

Restaurants: Agashiye (Ahmedabad), Vishalla.

Sweets: Fafda-Jalebi, Chaas, Mohanthal..

Artefact Conservation Centers

Overview: India’s apex institution for scientific conservation of cultural property.

History: Established in 1976 under the Archaeological Survey of India.

Best Time: November to February.

Specialities: Restoration of paintings, manuscripts, and antiquities.

Nearby Attractions: Rumi Darwaza, British Residency.

Food: Tunday Kababi, Wahid Biryani.

Restaurants: Oudhyana, Dastarkhwan.

Sweets: Makhan Malai, Thandai, Sheermal.
