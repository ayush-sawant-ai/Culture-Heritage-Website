Indian Film Heritage

Explore iconic institutions and studios shaping Indian cinema

Film City, Mumbai

Significance: A major hub for Bollywood film production and a landmark for Hindi cinema.

Location: Goregaon East, Mumbai, Maharashtra

Yash Raj Studios, Mumbai

Significance: One of the most renowned studios known for classic and contemporary Bollywood hits.

Location: Andheri West, Mumbai, Maharashtra

FTII (Film and Television Institute of India), Pune

Significance: India's premier film institute, producing top filmmakers and actors.

Location: Law College Road, Pune, Maharashtra

National Film Archive of India (NFAI), Pune

Significance: Preserves Indian film heritage and serves as a vital research hub.

Location: Law College Road, Pune, Maharashtra

Ramoji Film City, Hyderabad

Significance: Guinness World Record holder for the largest film studio complex in the world.

Location: Hayathnagar, Hyderabad, Telangana

Annapurna Studios, Hyderabad

Significance: A key hub for Telugu cinema production and post-production.

Location: Banjara Hills, Hyderabad, Telangana

Chennai Film Institute

Significance: One of the oldest government film institutes in India.

Location: CIT Campus, Chennai, Tamil Nadu

AVM Productions, Chennai

Significance: A legendary production house, part of South India’s film history since the 1940s.

Location: Vadapalani, Chennai, Tamil Nadu
