Archaeological Wonders of India

Mohenjo-daro

History: One of the largest cities of the Indus Valley Civilization, developed around 2500 BCE with advanced urban planning.

Best Time to Visit: October to February

Location: Sindh, Pakistan

Speciality: Great Bath, organized street system, baked brick buildings, advanced drainage.

Sanchi Stupa

History: Built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE, it's one of Indiaâ€™s oldest stone structures.

Best Time to Visit: November to March

Location: Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh, India

Speciality: Buddhist architecture, beautiful gateways (Toranas), and stone carvings depicting Jataka tales.

Hampi

History: Capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century, it was once a prosperous and grand city.

Best Time to Visit: October to February

Location: Karnataka, India

Speciality: Ruins of temples, stone chariots, royal enclosures, and sacred riverbank architecture.

Ellora Caves

History: A complex of 34 rock-cut temples built between the 6th and 10th centuries CE by Hindus, Jains, and Buddhists.

Best Time to Visit: November to March

Location: Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India

Speciality: Kailasa Temple (carved from a single rock), Buddhist monasteries, Jain shrines.
